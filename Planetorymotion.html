Planet Information




Mercury:

 Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun and the smallest in the solar system. It has no atmosphere to retain heat, which causes extreme temperature changes between day and night.



Venus:
 Venus is the hottest planet due to its thick atmosphere made of carbon dioxide. It traps heat through a strong greenhouse effect and is similar in size to Earth.



Earth:
 Earth is the only known planet that supports life. It has water, oxygen, and a suitable atmosphere, making it unique among all planets.



Mars:
 Mars is known as the Red Planet because of iron oxide on its surface. Scientists believe it may have had water in the past.



Jupiter:
 Jupiter is the largest planet in the solar system. It is a gas giant with a very strong storm called the Great Red Spot.



Saturn:
 Saturn is famous for its beautiful ring system. It is the second-largest planet and is mostly made of gases like hydrogen and helium.



Uranus:
 Uranus is a cold gas giant that rotates on its side. It has a pale blue color due to the presence of methane in its atmosphere.



Neptune:
 Neptune is the farthest planet from the Sun. It is known for its deep blue color and extremely strong winds.
